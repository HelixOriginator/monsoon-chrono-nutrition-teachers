Monsoon-Adaptive Chrono-Nutrition Protocol for Night-Owl Teachers

A simple, practical food and meal timing system designed for teachers who sleep late, teach long hours, and struggle with low energy, weak digestion, and monsoon-related fatigue.

Built for private school teachers in India facing humidity, infections, brain fog, and heavy digestion during July–September.

Core Daily Rules

  • Warm water immediately after waking
  • Heaviest meal at midday (your biological peak)
  • Light dinner before 8:30–9 p.m.
  • No cold or raw foods in monsoon
  • Use ginger, jeera, ajwain daily
  • Avoid caffeine after evening
  • Shift sleep earlier gradually, not forcefully

Sample Daily Schedule

Morning (Wake 9:30–10 a.m.)

  • Warm lemon-honey water
  • Fruit + soaked almonds

Main Meal (12:30–1:30 p.m.)

  • Moong dal + rice
  • Lauki/tori sabzi
  • Ghee + ginger

Evening Snack (4–5 p.m.)

  • Guava or roasted makhana
  • Tulsi-ginger tea

Dinner (7:30–8:30 p.m.)

  • Khichdi or light soup
  • Boiled vegetables
  • Buttermilk with jeera

90-Day Plan

  • Weeks 1–3: Reset digestion and reduce heaviness
  • Weeks 4–8: Build focus and immunity for teaching
  • Weeks 9–12: Gradually shift sleep earlier by 15–20 minutes per week

Expected Benefits

  • Steady classroom energy
  • Better digestion and less bloating
  • Fewer colds and infections
  • Reduced tea/coffee dependency
  • Improved sleep rhythm over time
